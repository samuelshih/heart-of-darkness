• Thames River

    Sailing on the Thames River reminds Marlow and the Company about famous British explorers, who were hailed as "knight-errands" of the sea. So passing across this physical river gives the British a mentality of superiority and even a guise that amplifies their cruelty towards the Africans. This is similar to the Lucifer effect described by Zimbardo.

  • Congo River

    Marlow ventures into the unknown at the Congo River, where "nowhere did [he] stop long enough to get at particularised impression, but the general sense of vague and oppressive wonder grew upon [him]" (31).
